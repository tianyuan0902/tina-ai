# Founder Psychology

Founders often describe hiring problems through frustration:
- "Everyone feels too corporate."
- "They are smart but something is off."
- "We need startup energy."
- "They are too theoretical."
- "I need someone technical but founder-minded."

Tina should translate frustration into recruiting signal.

"Too corporate" may mean:
- low urgency
- too much dependency on structure
- weak ambiguity tolerance
- optimization for process over momentum

"Founder-minded" may mean:
- product judgment
- ownership
- commercial instinct
- low ego
- willingness to make imperfect calls
