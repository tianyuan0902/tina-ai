# Levels.fyi Compensation Benchmarking

Source: https://www.levels.fyi/

Levels.fyi is useful for tech compensation benchmarking by company, role/title, career level, and location. It is especially useful when Tina needs to reason about software engineering, product, AI, data, infrastructure, or big-tech-adjacent markets where total compensation can include base salary, bonus, equity, and signing components.

## What It Helps Tina Reason About

- Whether a founder’s compensation expectation is competing with big tech, frontier AI labs, scaleups, or startups.
- Whether the role is being priced like a senior IC, staff-level operator, founding engineer, manager, or specialist.
- Whether the founder is anchoring on base salary when candidates are comparing total compensation.
- Whether location changes the talent pool or the expected pay band.
- Whether title and scope are mismatched: for example, asking for staff-level judgment while paying mid-level startup cash.

## How To Use It

Use Levels.fyi as directional market evidence. When Tina references this source, it should say the data is market benchmarking and should be cross-checked against role scope, company stage, equity liquidity, geography, and candidate profile.

## Limitations

- Data is crowdsourced and may skew toward candidates who choose to report compensation.
- Larger tech companies and well-known startups may be better represented than small private startups.
- Total compensation can overstate practical candidate value when equity is illiquid, volatile, or hard to compare across stages.
- Levels are not perfectly portable across companies; a Staff Engineer at one company may map differently elsewhere.

## Tina Pattern

When a founder asks what to pay, Tina should not just answer with a range. Tina should ask what market the company is trying to compete in.

Example:

"If this is a frontier AI/product engineer profile, I’d benchmark against total comp, not just base. The real question is whether you’re trying to win someone already operating at a top AI company, or a high-slope builder one step earlier."
