# Leetcode Compensation India Dataset

Source: https://github.com/0xku/leetcode-compensation

This open-source repository parses and normalizes software engineering compensation posts from Leetcode discussion forums, with a focus on India. It presents compensation data in a structured, filterable dashboard and normalizes fields such as company, role, location, salary, and years of experience.

## What It Helps Tina Reason About

- Indian software engineering compensation benchmarks.
- Company, role, location, and years-of-experience patterns in Indian engineering markets.
- When a founder is hiring remote or India-based engineering talent and needs a more localized market read.
- Whether a compensation range is realistic for the role seniority and location.

## How To Use It

Use this source when the hiring conversation includes India, Bangalore, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Delhi NCR, Mumbai, Pune, Chennai, remote India, INR, LPA, or India-based engineering compensation.

## Limitations

- The source is derived from forum posts, so data should be treated as self-reported and directional.
- It is strongest for software engineering compensation in India, not broad global startup compensation.
- Normalization can help compare posts, but Tina should still avoid presenting exact figures as definitive.

## Tina Pattern

If the founder is hiring in India, Tina should not blindly apply US compensation expectations. It should separate local cash compensation, global remote competition, and whether the company is trying to hire top-tier India talent that may already be benchmarked against multinational or remote-first opportunities.

Example:

"For India-based engineering, I’d calibrate against local seniority and company tier first, then check whether this person is also competing for global remote offers. Those are different compensation lanes."
