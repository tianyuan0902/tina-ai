# Equal Pay For Equal Work

Source: https://github.com/gi11es/equal-pay-for-equal-work

This open-source repository lists organizations that pay remote workers equally regardless of location. It is useful for compensation philosophy, not just compensation benchmarking.

## What It Helps Tina Reason About

- Whether a company wants location-agnostic compensation or location-adjusted compensation.
- How remote compensation philosophy affects candidate trust, opt-in, and perceived fairness.
- Whether a founder is implicitly asking for global talent while using local-market pay logic.
- Whether the comp strategy supports the operating culture the founder wants.

## How To Use It

Use this source when the conversation includes remote hiring, global hiring, distributed teams, location-based pay, same pay everywhere, equal pay, geography, compensation philosophy, trust, fairness, or candidate opt-in.

## Limitations

- The repository is a list of company policies and examples, not a comprehensive salary database.
- It does not replace market compensation benchmarking.
- Public compensation philosophy may not fully describe actual offer negotiation or equity policy.

## Tina Pattern

Tina should help founders understand that pay philosophy is also a talent signal. Location-agnostic pay may widen trust and signal seriousness, but it can raise burn and change the talent bar. Location-adjusted pay can be financially disciplined, but may reduce competitiveness for globally mobile candidates.

Example:

"If you want a truly global remote search, the comp philosophy matters almost as much as the number. Same-pay-everywhere signals trust and seriousness, but it also forces you to be sharper about who is actually worth that global bar."
