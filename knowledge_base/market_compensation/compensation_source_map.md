# Compensation Source Map

Use compensation sources as market calibration, not as truth. Tina should treat compensation data as directional evidence that helps a founder understand whether the role, seniority, market, geography, and expectations are aligned.

## How Tina Should Use Compensation Sources

- Separate base salary, bonus, equity, and total compensation whenever the source supports it.
- Ask whether the founder is competing against big tech, frontier AI labs, crypto/protocol teams, venture-backed startups, or local market employers.
- Treat self-reported salary data as useful but skewed toward people who choose to submit compensation.
- Treat public salary bands and equal-pay policies as stronger evidence of compensation philosophy than exact market median.
- Use compensation to surface tradeoffs: speed to hire, seniority expectations, remote geography, cash versus equity appetite, and whether the ask is over-scoped for the budget.

## Useful Source Types

- Levels.fyi: strong for company, title, location, level, and total compensation benchmarking in tech markets.
- Leetcode Compensation India dataset: useful for Indian software engineering compensation and role/company/location normalization, especially when hiring engineering talent in India.
- Equal Pay for Equal Work repository: useful for understanding remote-first compensation philosophy and location-agnostic pay policies.

## Calibration Language

Good Tina phrasing:

"I’d treat that comp range as a market signal, not a final answer. If you want someone who has already shipped this at a high-growth AI company, the market may behave closer to Levels-style total comp than local startup salary bands."

"The harder question is whether you want to pay for proven seniority or for high-slope talent with less proof. Those are different markets."

"If the company is remote-first, decide whether you are paying for the role or for the candidate’s location. That choice changes who opts in."

Avoid:

- claiming exact compensation without source context
- presenting crowdsourced data as authoritative
- ignoring equity liquidity and stage risk
- collapsing global, US, India, and remote markets into one range
