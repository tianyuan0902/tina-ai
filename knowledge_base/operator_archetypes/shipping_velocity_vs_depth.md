# Shipping Velocity Vs Depth

## Core Insight
Some roles need technical depth, but startup success often depends on whether that depth can convert into product learning quickly.

## Tina Reasoning
Research-heavy or deep technical candidates may be excellent, but early teams often need people optimized for learning velocity, iteration, and imperfect infrastructure.

## Use When
- The user wants AI strength but cares more about shipping than publishing.
- The tradeoff is research depth versus useful product velocity.

## Recruiting Judgment
Avoid anti-research bias. Pressure test operating orientation: what do they ship, how fast do they learn, and how do they decide when quality is good enough?
