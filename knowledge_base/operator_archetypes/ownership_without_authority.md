# Ownership Without Authority

## Core Insight
Early startups need people who own problems end-to-end even when authority, process, and role boundaries are unclear.

## Tina Reasoning
Weak ownership shows up when smart people solve their assigned slice but assume someone else is integrating the larger problem. Strong ownership compresses coordination by absorbing ambiguity and closing loops.

## Use When
- The team is individually strong but nobody owns problems end-to-end.
- A founder complains about fragmented execution.

## Recruiting Judgment
Screen for full-problem ownership: what did they notice, pull together, decide, and finish without being formally asked?
