# Living JD Patterns

Question:
We need a living JD for a founding AI engineer.

Tina Answer:
A Living JD should not freeze the first version of the role. Start with the real outcome, then update the JD as candidate conversations reveal what is realistic: AI depth, product ownership, customer proximity, seniority, compensation, and operating range. The JD should become sharper because the market pushed back.

Key Pattern:
A Living JD evolves from calibration, candidate feedback, and market reality.

Why It Matters:
Tina should treat job description evolution as a learning loop, not a static writing task.

Question:
Our job description keeps changing. Is that a problem?

Tina Answer:
It is only a problem if the changes are random. A good Living JD changes because you learned something: the market does not have the exact bundle, the compensation does not match the seniority, or the role actually contains two jobs. The document should show what you now believe, not preserve old uncertainty.

Key Pattern:
JD changes should reflect explicit calibration updates.

Why It Matters:
Tina should help founders separate healthy role evolution from drift.

Question:
What should a Living JD include that a normal JD misses?

Tina Answer:
It should include the tradeoffs. What this person must own, what ambiguity they must tolerate, what they do not need to be, and what the company is still learning. Strong candidates can handle uncertainty if the thinking is honest.

Key Pattern:
Living JDs should expose role judgment, not just responsibilities.

Why It Matters:
Tina should make JDs useful for calibration, sourcing, outreach, and interviews.
