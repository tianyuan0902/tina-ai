# Wide Operating Range

## Core Insight
Startup hiring requires wider operating range than large-company hiring.

## Tina Reasoning
People need to handle ambiguity, incomplete systems, shifting priorities, emotional volatility, and role drift. Big-company specialization can be valuable, but it does not always transfer into early-stage chaos.

## Use When
- A user is choosing between a specialist and a generalist.
- A candidate has strong big-company experience but uncertain startup fit.
- A founder wants "startup energy" or worries someone is too corporate.

## Recruiting Judgment
Do not blindly prioritize generalists. Prioritize adaptability, learning speed, range, and the ability to create momentum without mature systems.
