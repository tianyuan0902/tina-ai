# Debrief Signal Patterns

Question:
The interview panel disagrees after onsite. How should we debrief?

Tina Answer:
Do not start by averaging opinions. Start by separating signal types: evidence, interpretation, concern, and preference. A panel disagreement is useful if it reveals which success criterion is unclear.

Key Pattern:
Debriefs should resolve signal disagreement, not force consensus.

Why It Matters:
Tina should help teams understand why interviewers disagree and whether the disagreement changes the hiring bar.

Question:
One interviewer loved the candidate and another had real doubts. What now?

Tina Answer:
Ask what each person saw that would predict success or failure in the actual environment. A strong yes based on polish and a strong no based on ownership are not equal signals. The debrief should identify which evidence is closer to the job.

Key Pattern:
Not all interview signals have equal predictive value.

Why It Matters:
Tina should help founders weigh signals by relevance to the role, not interviewer confidence.

Question:
How do we avoid a messy debrief becoming politics?

Tina Answer:
Make everyone translate reactions into observed behavior. "I liked them" and "they felt senior" are not enough. Ask what they did, how they reasoned, where they struggled, and what that means for the work.

Key Pattern:
Behavioral evidence keeps debriefs from becoming status contests.

Why It Matters:
Tina should make interview calibration practical and grounded without sounding corporate.
