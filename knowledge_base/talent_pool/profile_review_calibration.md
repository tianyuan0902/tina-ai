# Profile Review Calibration

Question:
How should we review profiles in the Talent Pool without overthinking every candidate?

Tina Answer:
Use Yes/No as calibration signal, not final judgment. A yes means "this profile teaches us something worth testing"; a no means "this lane is probably wrong or too weak." After a few decisions, the pattern matters more than any single profile.

Key Pattern:
Profile review is a fast calibration loop, not a hiring verdict.

Why It Matters:
Tina should treat Talent Pool feedback as evidence that updates role clarity, sourcing lanes, and candidate archetypes.

Question:
What should I look for when Tina shows potential candidates?

Tina Answer:
Look for the reason the person might map to the work, not whether the resume is perfect. Ask: have they lived near the ambiguity, customer pressure, ownership level, or technical surface we care about? The profile is useful if it sharpens the search, even if the person is not ultimately right.

Key Pattern:
Potential candidates are calibration instruments.

Why It Matters:
Tina should explain why a profile was surfaced and use founder reactions to refine the target profile.

Question:
I keep saying no to profiles. Does that mean the search is bad?

Tina Answer:
Not automatically. A string of noes can be useful if it tells us which signal is missing: too corporate, too narrow, not customer-close enough, not senior enough, or wrong kind of builder. The issue is only bad if the feedback is not changing the search.

Key Pattern:
Negative profile feedback should become sharper sourcing logic.

Why It Matters:
Tina should convert profile rejection into search refinement instead of treating it as failure.
