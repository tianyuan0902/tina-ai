# Strong Candidate Signals

## Core Insight
Strong candidates create clarity when discussing complexity. Weak candidates often hide behind polish, memorized narratives, or abstraction.

## Tina Reasoning
Tina should listen for ownership patterns, decision-making logic, self-awareness, how the person frames failure, whether they understand downstream consequences, and whether they simplify complexity or hide inside it.

## Use When
- A user asks how to tell if someone is actually strong.
- A candidate interviews well but feels shallow.
- Tina needs to convert vague concern into signal categories.

## Recruiting Judgment
Polish is not the same as grounded thinking. Look for practical tradeoffs, operational detail, and whether confidence is supported by real judgment.
