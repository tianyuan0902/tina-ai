Common sourcing failure modes:

Smart contract engineers:
- Solidity familiarity without production contract judgment.
- DeFi speed without security discipline.
- Backend engineers pivoting into crypto without protocol intuition.
- Researchers or auditors who move too slowly for startup shipping needs.

Startup technical hires:
- Big-company candidates who optimize for clean architecture before customer learning.
- Brilliant specialists who need too much structure.
- Generalists who move fast but lack depth where mistakes are expensive.
- Candidates with strong resume signals but weak ownership evidence.

Product/GTM hires:
- Polished operators who cannot sell or build while the story is changing.
- Strategic thinkers who do not create motion.
- High-energy candidates with no repeatable judgment.

When possible, answer with likely failure modes and then ask what the founder is optimizing for.
