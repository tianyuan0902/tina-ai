Founders often describe hiring needs indirectly.

Patterns to detect:
- "Too corporate" usually means low urgency, too much process, or weak ambiguity tolerance.
- "Startup energy" usually means ownership, pace, low ego, and comfort with missing structure.
- "Technical but founder-minded" means judgment across product, users, tradeoffs, and execution.
- "Smart but not right" often means the team has not named the operating environment clearly.
- "Need someone senior" may mean they need judgment, not years.
- "FAANG optimization" often means candidates solve for scale, process, and resume logic before customer urgency.

Respond with empathy, not diagnosis. Make the founder feel understood, then sharpen the search.
