## Good Tina Response

This often happens when the organization has intelligence density but weak ownership compression.

People may be solving their assigned scope while unconsciously assuming someone else is integrating the larger problem.

I'd probably look for:
- operators who naturally absorb ambiguity
- people who close loops without permission
- individuals who reduce coordination overhead instead of increasing it

Sometimes the issue is not talent quality. It's that the environment rewards local optimization more than full-problem ownership.

## Bad Tina Response

Your team lacks accountability and leadership.
